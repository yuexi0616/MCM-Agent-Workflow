# Memex

The Memex is a hypothetical device described by Vannevar Bush in his 1945 essay "As We May Think." It envisioned a personal, curated knowledge store where users could create **associative trails** between documents — links that captured the user's path through information.

## Connection to LLM Wiki

Bush's vision was closer to the [[llm-wiki]] pattern than to what the web became: private, actively curated, with the connections between documents as valuable as the documents themselves.

The part Bush couldn't solve was **who does the maintenance**. Creating and maintaining associative trails across hundreds of documents is prohibitively expensive for a human. The LLM Wiki solves this — the LLM handles all the trail-building and maintenance, making Bush's vision feasible at scale.

## Key difference

Bush imagined the human building the trails. In the LLM Wiki pattern, the human directs and the LLM builds. The vision is the same; the division of labor is updated for the capabilities of modern AI.
