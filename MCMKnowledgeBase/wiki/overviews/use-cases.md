# LLM Wiki Use Cases

The [[llm-wiki]] pattern generalizes across any domain where knowledge accumulates over time.

## Personal knowledge management

Tracking goals, health, psychology, self-improvement. Filing journal entries, articles, podcast notes. Building a structured picture of yourself over time.

## Academic research

Going deep on a topic over weeks or months. Reading papers, articles, reports. Incrementally building a comprehensive wiki with an evolving thesis.

## Book companion wikis

Filing each chapter as you go, building pages for characters, themes, plot threads. By the end, a rich companion wiki. Analogous to fan wikis like Tolkien Gateway — thousands of interlinked pages built by volunteers. The LLM does the same work personally and automatically.

## Business and team wikis

An internal wiki maintained by LLMs, fed by Slack threads, meeting transcripts, project documents, customer calls. Optionally with humans in the loop reviewing updates. The wiki stays current because the LLM does the maintenance no one on the team wants to do.

## Other applications

Competitive analysis, due diligence, trip planning, course notes, hobby deep-dives — anything where accumulating and organizing knowledge over time beats keeping it scattered.
